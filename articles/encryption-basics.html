Introduction to Encryption Technology

VPN and Encryption Technology Introduction

Encryption technology is the core of protecting the security of internet communication, and VPN (Virtual Private Network) helps users ensure the privacy and security of online activities through encryption technology. Below is the importance of encryption technology and the role of VPN:

1. Encryption Protects Data Privacy - Encryption technology converts data into an unreadable format, ensuring that even if data is stolen, hackers cannot decipher it. VPN uses encryption protocols (such as AES) to encrypt users' network traffic, protecting personal information and online activities.

2. Preventing Data Leakage - VPN protects user data from being stolen on public Wi-Fi networks through encryption, preventing hackers from obtaining sensitive information through man-in-the-middle attacks. This allows users to browse the web and conduct online transactions safely in insecure network environments.

3. Enhancing Network Security - VPN not only encrypts user data but also increases anonymity by hiding the real IP address. This protective measure effectively reduces the risk of being tracked, monitored, or subjected to malicious attacks, ensuring users' network security.
